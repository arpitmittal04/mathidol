About Math Idol

Math Idol is introduced as a tribute to the legendary genius in Mathematics, Late Shakuntala Devi and is dedicated to the goal of strengthening the mathematical capabilities of our nation’s youth. We believe that one way to meet this goal is to identify, recognize, and reward excellence in mathematics through a multi-city contest which will be conducted in several stages.

The purpose of this competition is to introduce youngsters of Class 10 to math challenges in a fun and enjoyable way, thus inspiring their further interest and advancement in mathematics. All students will have an opportunity to participate in these problem solving and enriching mathematics experiences. The contests are designed to engage students in effective mathematical problem solving that is consistent with curricular goals, and to prepare students for future mathematical success.

Shakuntala Devi

The
Human Computer

Shakuntala Devi (1929-2013) was a Bangalore-based Mathematics prodigy. With a divine skill in numbers from the age of three, she has been stunning the world with her uncanny skill in computing the most intricate problems mentally. In fact, she was even quicker than highly sophisticated computers. More reasons why she was called a “Human Computer”

She received Gold Medal from Philippine University in 1969 and the Ramanujan Mathematical Genius Award in Washington D C in 1988. She was also the author of several books including “Book of Numbers”, “Figuring - the Joy of Numbers”, “Mathability”, and “In the Wonderland of Numbers”.

She started the Shakuntala Devi Educational Foundation Public Trust in order to spread her love for mathematics and to nurture excellence in mathematics
